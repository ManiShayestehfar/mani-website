This is a note on a report made as a part of my Denison Scholarship. The torsion of a curve in Euclidean space quantifies how much the curve twists. Geometric phase is a phenomenon that can explain how a cat falling with no angular momentum can still land of its feet. This paper generalises the work of Bhattecharjee et al. (1992) with detailed mathematical rigour, and leads to insight to the relationship between the choice of frame bundles, connections, and the consequent geometric phase that appears from their configurations.
