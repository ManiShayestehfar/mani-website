The nonlinear Schrödinger equation (NLSE) models optical solitons and can be extended to include higher-order dispersion, enabling advances in ultra-fast lasers and optical communications. This project applies Lie symmetry methods to analyse previously unexplored higher-order NLSE variants, deriving all solutions from a single known solution. We show that pure quartic and arbitrary pure higher-order solitons each possess exactly two Lie symmetries, both previously observed experimentally, and prove that no additional solutions exist for these cases, providing a foundation for future studies of more general higher-order NLSE models.
